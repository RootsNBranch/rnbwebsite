Roots N Branch Siva Academy

At RNB, we blend traditional Samoan dance forms like Siva and Fa’ataupati (Roots) with contemporary dance styles (Branch). Our choreography mixes vibrant modern beats with timeless Samoan songs—keeping culture alive, relevant, and powerful for every generation.

Kids Siva

Introduces our young ones to the beauty of Samoan dance through movements, timing and storytelling. It's designed to help them grow in confidence, cultural identity and group connection in a fun and safe space.

Duration: 45 min

Age Group: 5–12 yrs

Teen to Adult Siva (Males Only)

Focus on stamina, tradition & precise movements.

Music: Mixture of old Samoan, modern Samoan music and drumming

Duration: 60 min

Age Group: 13+ yrs

Siva X

Siva X is using Samoan movements with upbeat current music creating a freedom of Xpression—a twist that still incorporates the Samoan movements, for example a fa’ataupati or siva Samoa—bridging the past with the present, flaunting the branches while respecting the roots.

Focus: Stamina, Samoan movements, expression and fun

Music: High energy fusion, mixture of all genres that are upbeat

Duration: 60 min

Open To: 13+ males

Siva Fit

High energy, sweat-dripping, endorphin-boosting fitness using Samoan dance. No two classes are the same.

Focus: Cardio, strength and stamina through movement

Music: Fusion of Island beats and workout hits

Duration: 45 min

All fitness levels welcome

RNB Showcase Crew

This is a performance-based class for those wanting to take their siva to the next level. Choreography heavy and pushing creative boundaries through movement, storytelling and stage presence.

Potential to perform onstage at special events, exposure to the entertainment industry and key leaders and performers of not only our community but internationally.

Duration: 60 min

Open To: Invitation or audition based

Culture Workshop

Culture Workshop is a chance for RNB students and community to connect to other Polynesian, Melanesian and Micronesian cultures. RNB works closely with community leaders and performers to teach about their culture and dance. This gives an outlook on other cultures and a way to learn how similar and how different customs and way of life is for different cultures.

Frequency: Monthly or special event based
